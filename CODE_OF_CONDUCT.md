# Code of Conduct

The Resonate community — across GitHub, Discord, and any official channel — is a place to learn, build, and ship durable systems together. This document sets the floor for how we treat each other.

## What we expect

- **Respect the people, not just the work.** Critique code, designs, and ideas — not the person who wrote them.
- **Assume good intent.** Most disagreements are misreadings. Ask before escalating.
- **Be patient with newcomers.** Everyone is new to durable execution at some point. Answer the question first; correct the framing second.
- **Keep it on-topic.** Resonate spaces exist for Resonate work. Off-topic threads belong elsewhere.
- **Use clear, professional language.** Strong opinions are fine. Personal attacks are not.

## What's not allowed

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination based on identity (race, gender, sexuality, disability, religion, nationality, or anything else that makes a person who they are).
- Sustained disruption of discussions, threads, or events.
- Sexualized language or imagery in any Resonate space.
- Doxxing or sharing others' private information without consent.
- Persistent unwanted contact after someone has asked you to stop.

## Reporting

If you see or experience behavior that violates this code:

- **GitHub:** open a private security advisory on this repo, or email the maintainers (see [SECURITY.md](./SECURITY.md) for the contact path).
- **Discord:** DM a moderator (any member with the `@moderator` role).

Reports are handled confidentially. We will never share your identity with the person reported without your consent.

## Enforcement

Maintainers and Discord moderators are responsible for enforcement. Responses may include:

1. A private warning explaining what was wrong and what we expect going forward.
2. A temporary ban from the relevant space (Discord, repo, etc.).
3. A permanent ban for repeated or severe violations.

Decisions are made by the maintainer team. We aim to be fair, prompt, and clear about why we took the action we did.
